101 Most Useful Websites

  1. archive.is — take a permanent snapshot of a web page that will exist forever even if the original page is gone.
  2. autodraw.com — create freehand doodles and watch them magically transform into beautiful drawings powered by machine learning.
  3. fast.com — check the current speed of your Internet connection.
  4. slides.com — create pixel-perfect slide decks and broadcast your presentations to an audience of any size from anywhere.
  5. screenshot.guru — take high-resolution screenshots of web pages on mobile and desktops.
  6. dictation.io — accurate and quick voice recognition in your browser using Google Speech API.
  7. reverse.photos — upload an image and find similar pictures on the web.
  8. copychar.cc — copy special characters and emojis that aren’t on your keyboard.
  9. codeacademy.com — the best place to learn coding online.
  10. noisli.com — ambient noises to help you improve focus and boost productivity.
  11. iconfinder.com — millions of icons for all kinds of projects. Also try icons8.com and flaticon.com.
  12. jotti.org — scan any suspicious file or email attachment for viruses.
  13. wolframalpha.com — gets answers directly without searching
  14. earth.google.com — explore beautiful cities, landmarks and orbit the world in 3D from the comfort of your browser.
  15. unsplash.com — the best place to download images absolutely free.
  16. videos.pexels.com — an online library of free HD videos you can use everywhere. Also see videvo.net.
  17. invideo.io — make impressive videos and branded stories for Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube. Also see animoto.com and biteable.com.
  18. semrush.com — Analyze your competitor’s website and discover who is sending them traffic.
  19. zohosign.com — Sign documents online with legally binding e-signatures.
  20. everytimezone.com — a less confusing view of the world time zones.
  21. e.ggtimer.com — a simple online timer for your daily needs.
  22. random.org — pick random numbers, flip coins, and more.
  23. remove.bg — remove the background from any photograph without firing up Photoshop. Also see unscreen.com for removing the background from GIFs and videos.
  24. myfonts.com/WhatTheFont — upload an image of any text and quickly determine the font family.
  25. fonts.google.com — the best collection of open source fonts that you can use anywhere without restrictions.
  26. fontstruct.com — draw and build your own fonts and use them in any application.
  27. calligraphr.com — transform your handwriting into a real font.
  28. wikibooks.org — A giant collection of online textbooks that anyone can edit. You can find books on various subjects, from mathematics to languages, and download them for free.
  29. hyperhistory.com — A visual timeline of 3,000 years of world history. You can explore different periods, regions, and topics, and see how they are connected.
  30. tineye.com — A reverse image search engine that lets you see where an image is being used on the web. You can upload an image or enter its URL, and TinEye will show you similar images, websites that use it, and other information.
  31. digitalhistory.uh.edu — A comprehensive resource for learning about U.S. history. It has primary sources, quizzes, timelines, maps, and more. You can also browse by themes, eras, or groups.
  32. waybackmachine.org — A digital archive of the web that lets you access old versions of web pages. You can enter a URL and see how it looked in the past, or browse through collections of historical websites.
  33. projectgutenberg.org — A huge database of tens of thousands of free downloadable books. You can read them online or on your e-reader. The books are mostly classics and public domain works, in various languages.
  34. manybooks.net — Another site that offers free books in different formats and languages. You can also browse by genres, authors, or popularity, and read reviews from other readers.
  35. owl.purdue.edu — A fantastic site that provides free resources for improving your writing. It has guides on grammar, style, citation, research, and more. It also has an updated MLA style guide.
  36. librarything.com — A site that lets you share what you're reading with millions of other users. You can catalog your books, join groups, participate in discussions, and get recommendations.
  37. hemingwayapp.com — A site that helps you make your writing clearer and more effective. You can paste your text and see the readability score, suggested edits, and other feedback.
  38. freevee.com — A site that shows you free, ad-supported movies that you can watch on Amazon Prime Video. You just need an Amazon account to access them. You can browse by genres, ratings, or popularity.
  39. justwatch.com — A site that helps you find where to watch movies and TV shows online. You can search by title, genre, or provider, and see the cheapest or free options. It also shows you the latest releases and popular titles.
  40. zamzar.com — A site that lets you convert any media file format to another format for free. You can upload your file or enter its URL, choose the output format, and download the converted file. It supports hundreds of formats, including audio, video, image, document, and more.
  41. impawards.com — A site that showcases the latest and best movie posters. You can see the posters in high resolution, and browse by categories, genres, or years. It also has archives going back to 1912.
  42. animoto.com — A site that lets you make your own videos using your images and music. You can choose from various templates, effects, and styles, and share your videos online. It's free for basic features, but you can upgrade for more options.
  43. imsdb.com — A site that lets you access thousands of movie scripts for free. You can read them online or download them as text files. You can also browse by genres, titles, or writers.
  44. pdfescape.com — A site that lets you edit PDF files online for free. You can add text, images, links, annotations, and more. You can also fill out forms, password protect, and print your PDFs.
  45. pixlr.com — A site that lets you edit photos online for free. You can use advanced tools, filters, effects, and more. You can also create collages, graphics, and logos.
  46. canva.com — A site that lets you design stunning graphics online for free. You can use templates, icons, fonts, and more. You can also create flyers, posters, logos, and social media posts.
  47. draw.io — A site that lets you create diagrams online for free. You can use shapes, connectors, symbols, and more. You can also export your diagrams as images or PDFs.
  48. sketch.io — A site that lets you draw and paint online for free. You can use brushes, layers, filters, and more. You can also import and export your drawings as images.
  49. duolingo.com — A site that lets you learn languages online for free. You can choose from dozens of languages, and practice with interactive lessons, games, and stories. You can also track your progress and earn rewards.
  50. memrise.com — Another site that lets you learn languages online for free. You can choose from hundreds of languages, and learn with flashcards, videos, and quizzes. You can also create your own courses and join communities.
  51. quizlet.com — A site that lets you study anything online for free. You can create your own flashcards, games, and quizzes, or use the ones created by other users. You can also join classes and groups.
  52. khanacademy.org — A site that lets you learn anything online for free. You can choose from thousands of courses, videos, and exercises on various subjects, from math to art. You can also track your progress and earn badges.
  53. coursera.org — A site that lets you take online courses from top universities and organizations for free. You can choose from hundreds of courses on various topics, from business to health. You can also earn certificates and degrees.
  54. ted.com — A site that lets you watch inspiring and informative talks from experts and innovators for free. You can choose from thousands of videos on various themes, from science to creativity. You can also join communities and events.
  55. medium.com — A site that lets you read and write stories online for free. You can choose from millions of articles on various topics, from personal to professional. You can also follow your favorite writers and publications.
  56. pocket.com — A site that lets you save and read articles online for free. You can save articles from any website or app, and read them later on any device. You can also discover new articles and recommendations.
  57. feedly.com — A site that lets you organize and read online content for free. You can follow your favorite blogs, websites, podcasts, and more, and read them in one place. You can also discover new sources and topics.
  58. reddit.com — A site that lets you join and participate in online communities for free. You can choose from thousands of subreddits on various topics, from gaming to politics. You can also post, comment, vote, and chat with other users.
  59. quora.com — A site that lets you ask and answer questions online for free. You can choose from millions of questions on various topics, from personal to professional. You can also follow your favorite topics and people.
  60. stackoverflow.com — A site that lets you ask and answer programming questions online for free.
